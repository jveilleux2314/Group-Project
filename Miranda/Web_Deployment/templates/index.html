How popular is K-pop?

Use the interactive charts to explore 10 years of music data

K-Pop is short for Korean popular music, and its rise in popularity - as well as the dedication of its fans - has taken social media by storm. The cultural impact of K-Pop fans was even highlighted by CNN in 2020, documenting the political activism of fans of Korean pop idol groups, and is driving international sales by groups like BTS partnering with brands like McDonalds. With as much social and purchasing power as these fans wield, will K-pop artists have staying power and therefore the communities surrounding them?

So how popular is K-Pop? Looking at song data from 2010 - 2020 from Spotify and the Hot 100 Billboard, an analysis of popularity is made. Browse the interactive charts to compare how K-Pop ranks in the most recognized music genres: Country, Dance, Hip-Hop, Latin, Pop, and Rock. Using Spotify’s audio features that mark acousticness, danceability, energy, instrumentalness, liveness, loudness, speechiness, and tempo, the machine learning model predicts if certain features are more likely to cause a song or genre to be popular. And if so, does K-Pop hit all the right marks for a hit on the Hot 100 Billboard?
